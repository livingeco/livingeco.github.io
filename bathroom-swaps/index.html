Sustainable Hygiene

10 Eco-Friendly Bathroom Swaps
You Can Make This Week

Simple, plastic-free alternatives for the most wasteful room in your home.

🌿 10 swaps · 💰 Most save money long-term · ♻️ Zero learning curve

Why the Bathroom?

The Most Overlooked Room in Your Home

Most people think about the kitchen first when going zero-waste: reusable bags, glass jars, bamboo utensils. But the bathroom is quietly one of the biggest sources of single-use plastic in the home.

Think about everything you go through in a year: shampoo bottles, conditioner bottles, face wash, razors, cotton pads, toothbrushes, deodorant sticks. Nearly all of it comes in plastic, and almost none of it gets recycled.

The good news? The bathroom is also one of the easiest rooms to overhaul. Most swaps cost the same as or less than what you are already buying, and many of them actually work better.

Here are 10 swaps you can make this week, starting with the simplest ones and working up to the ones that take a little more adjustment.

You don't need to do all 10 at once. Start with one. When you run out of your current product, swap it for the eco version. That's it.

The Swaps

10 Simple Switches

Each one is a direct, like-for-like replacement. No lifestyle overhaul required.

01

Bamboo Toothbrush

Replace: Plastic toothbrush

The average person uses 300 plastic toothbrushes in their lifetime and almost none of them ever decompose. Bamboo toothbrushes have a biodegradable handle and work just as well as plastic ones. Simply remove the bristles before composting the handle.

Tip

Look for ones with BPA-free nylon bristles. Replace every 3 months, just like a regular toothbrush.

Impact

Saves about 4 plastic toothbrushes per year, per person.

02

Shampoo Bar and Conditioner Bar

Replace: Plastic shampoo and conditioner bottles

Over 550 million shampoo bottles end up in landfill every year. A shampoo bar lasts as long as 2 to 3 bottles, has zero plastic packaging, and is compact enough to take anywhere. Many people find their hair actually improves once it adjusts.

Tip

Give your hair a 2 to 3 week transition period when switching. Hard water can make bars less effective, so a diluted apple cider vinegar rinse helps in those areas.

Impact

Replaces 4 to 6 plastic bottles per year.

03

Safety Razor

Replace: Disposable plastic razors

Disposable razors are made of mixed plastics and metal, which makes them completely unrecyclable. A safety razor is a one-time purchase that lasts years or even decades. Only the thin metal blade is replaced, and blades are 100% recyclable.

Tip

Blades are incredibly cheap, around £5 to £10 for 100. Start with a mild, forgiving razor if you are new to it.

Impact

Saves hundreds of plastic razors over a lifetime.

04

Reusable Cotton Rounds / Facial Pads

Replace: Disposable cotton pads

Cotton pads are used for about 30 seconds and then thrown away. Reusable pads made from organic cotton or bamboo do the exact same job, wash beautifully, and last for years. They come in mesh laundry bags for easy washing.

Tip

Keep a small wet bag next to your sink to collect used pads between washes.

Impact

Replaces 700 to 1000 or more disposable pads per year.

05

Refillable Deodorant

Replace: Single-use plastic deodorant sticks

Most plastic deodorant sticks cannot be recycled. Refillable deodorants use the same outer case indefinitely and you simply swap in a new refill. Natural deodorants also tend to use cleaner, skin-friendly ingredients.

Tip

Natural deodorants may need a 2 to 4 week adjustment period as your body recalibrates. Stick with it.

Impact

Cuts deodorant plastic waste by up to 90%.

06

Solid Facial Cleanser or Cleansing Balm in Glass

Replace: Face wash in plastic bottles

Liquid face washes are mostly water wrapped in plastic. Solid facial cleansers are concentrated, plastic-free, and last much longer. Alternatively, look for face washes in glass jars with metal lids, which are infinitely recyclable.

Tip

Store your solid cleanser on a soap dish that drains well to extend its life.

Impact

Eliminates 2 to 4 plastic bottles per year.

07

Bamboo or Stainless Steel Hairbrush

Replace: Plastic hairbrush

Plastic hairbrushes are nearly impossible to recycle. Bamboo-handled brushes with natural bristles are biodegradable, gentle on the scalp, and often better for reducing frizz. Stainless steel brushes are virtually indestructible.

Tip

Clean your brush weekly by removing trapped hair and gently washing the base with a little diluted shampoo.

Impact

One brush can last 5 to 10 or more years versus replacing plastic ones every year or two.

08

Bar Soap (Naked / Unwrapped)

Replace: Liquid soap in plastic pump bottles

Liquid soap uses up to 5 times more energy to produce than bar soap and comes in plastic that is rarely recycled. A simple, unwrapped bar of soap, or one wrapped in paper, is one of the easiest and cheapest swaps you can make.

Tip

Use a wooden or ceramic soap dish to keep the bar dry between uses. It will last much longer.

Impact

Replaces 3 to 5 plastic pump bottles per year.

09

Menstrual Cup or Reusable Period Products

Replace: Disposable pads and tampons

The average person uses between 5,000 and 15,000 disposable period products in their lifetime, most of which contain plastics and end up in landfill or waterways. A menstrual cup lasts up to 10 years. Reusable pads and period underwear are equally low-waste alternatives.

Tip

Menstrual cups have a learning curve, so give yourself 2 to 3 cycles to get comfortable. Period underwear is a great low-commitment starting point.

Impact

Potentially eliminates thousands of single-use items over a lifetime.

10

Plastic-Free Toilet Paper

Replace: Plastic-wrapped toilet roll

Most toilet paper arrives wrapped in plastic film that goes straight to landfill. Bamboo toilet paper is softer, more sustainable than wood pulp, and almost always comes wrapped in paper or cardboard packaging. Some brands also plant trees with every purchase.

Tip

Buy in bulk to reduce packaging further and save money per roll.

Impact

Removes significant plastic from your weekly shop.

Getting Started

Where to Begin

If you are just beginning your bathroom zero-waste journey, here is a simple order to follow. Start with the items you run out of first, and replace each one as it empties.

  • 1Bar soap: easiest swap, costs less, no adjustment needed
  • 2Bamboo toothbrush: direct replacement, zero learning curve
  • 3Reusable cotton rounds: game changer for your skincare routine
  • 4Shampoo bar: biggest visual impact on plastic reduction
  • 5Safety razor: upfront cost pays off within 2 to 3 months

A Note on Budget

Many people assume eco-friendly products cost more. Some do have a higher upfront cost (a safety razor, for example), but almost all of them work out cheaper over time because they last so much longer.

A set of reusable cotton rounds costs around £8 to £12 and replaces £30 to £40 worth of disposable pads per year. A shampoo bar replaces 2 to 3 bottles. A safety razor pays itself back in blade savings within a few months.

Going plastic-free in the bathroom is not just better for the planet. Over the course of a year, it is almost always better for your wallet too.

Final Thoughts

The bathroom generates more single-use plastic than almost any other room in the home, but it is also one of the most satisfying places to make changes. The products are simple, the swaps are easy to find, and the results are immediate.

You do not need to overhaul everything at once. Pick one swap this week. Notice how easy it was. Then pick another one next month.

That is how sustainable living actually works: not a dramatic overnight transformation, but a quiet, steady series of better choices that add up to something meaningful over time.

Sustainability isn't about perfection. Every swap you make, however small, genuinely matters.
